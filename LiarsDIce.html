Game Instructions

  • Name yourself, and pick the number of opponents between 1 and 9.
  • You play first.
  • Your hand is displayed below your player name.
  • You place a FACE VALUE bet (between 1-6, the faces of a die)
  • You also place a NUMBER bet, representing how many dice with your face value are on the table.
  • After the initial bet, each player must either raise the number of dice in their bet, or, keep the previous number, but raise the face value (i.e. from 4 4's to 4 6's).
  • You may call bluff on other players if you think there are less dice then they claim on the table, and they may do so to you.
  • You may also call spot on if you think their bet was exactly right.
  • If you don't think the opposing player is bluffing or spot on, you can pass and have the next player play.
  • When a player is challenged (called out as bluffing or spot on), the challenger looses a die if the better was correct, or the better looses a die if they were right.
  • After every challenge, the round restarts with one less die in the losers hand, the last player with dice in their hand wins.
